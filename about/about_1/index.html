About me (all text)

April 8, 2024

About me

My name is Pengfei Yin (殷鹏飞). I completed my BSc at the Ocean University of China, where I was deeply attracted to how mechanisms underlying molecular biology are discovered. Since then, I have been systematically enhancing my ability to decipher biological mechanisms. In addition to experiments, numerous scientific studies demonstrate that Physics (Francis Crick) and computational analysis can also help us understand the biological phenomena. Thus, I have developed a background in molecular biology, complemented by training in mathematical modeling and core courses for Physics students (including the 3 volumes of the Feynman Lectures on Physics).

After graduation, I joined the Meizhen Zheng’s Lab in Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech) to study 3D genomics. My thesis project is to use computational and theoretical method to explore dynamic 3D chromatin architecture of active and inactive regions and gene regulation of Drosophila under heat stress.

What do I want to engage in ?

I want to engage in Research and Education

Motivation about Research

  1. Enjoy explaining phenomena and figuring out mechanisms.
  2. Research can fulfill my desire of exploration over the long term.

Research interests

  1. Genomics (or how the 3D genome is organized into different layers and how it is involved with gene expression and regulation); (Any topic related to gene expression and regulation deserves our attention. The more details we understand about these mechanisms, the better we can utilize them for the benefit of human society.)
  2. Single-cell, developmental biology; (We often notice some specific mechanism and study it through comparisons: either by comparing natural occurrences or through perturbations. Different developmental stages, different cell cycle stages, different cell populations, and different species (if the time scale is large enough, it’s evolution) are natural comparisons.)
  3. Computational & theoretical methods.

Motivation about Education

  1. I didn’t start to think about what I wanted to pursue until college. I guess this is also the case for many other students, especially those from underdeveloped areas without good family education.
  2. I think education may be the root solution for many social issues.
  3. I use myself as a case study to see what level I can achieve.

How do I improve myself?

I improve myself from 3 aspects: qualities, ways of thinking, and basic knowledge

Qualities

Curious, meticulous, cooperative, full of passion

Ways of thinking

  1. The essense of creativities (coming up new ideas), I think, is the abilities to come up with C using fact A and fact B.
  2. The first principle/reductionism: (1) Trace the history of some scientifc topic or technique back to its origin, so we can learn from other scientists about how to come up with C using fact A and B. (2) Unify lots of facts using simple rules or the very fundamental laws.
  3. The majority of biological phenomena can be divided into continuous stages. It’s not surprising that each key stage has its own mechanism to be explored which can be the potential research topics.

Basic knowledge

  1. I have a background in Physics and Math, which supports me to understand and learn things quickly and in depth.
  2. I build a framework of genomics so that I know lots of biological facts and quickly acquire some other necessary facts, which supports me to explain the data and put up a mechanism.
  3. A programming language . （In the era of the ChatGPT, we don’t need to remember so many details. Ways of thinking and a framework of some research field will be more important. Besides, a deep insight of the essenece of a biological process or phenomenon will help us solve important questions. Lots of facts show that Physics and Math can help it.）

Skills

  1. My background in genomics, combined with physics and mathematics, enhances analytical capabilities and my understanding of biological phenomena.
  2. Python, Linux, high-performance computing (HPC); Run and construct pipelines using snakemake(like ChIA-PET, RNA-seq, ChIP-seq/ATAC-seq); Data analysis in multi-omics (Some projects are in my github https://github.com/astudentfromsustech)
  3. Mathematical modelling; Modify algorithms (like Faster RCNN) according to our own needs; Machine learning (Machine learning is good at pattern recognition and classification.
