Draw figures for publications

March 11, 2024

  • publication
  • figures

Why?

Usually, I use Python with ggplot style to draw plots and take screenshots of date displayed in browser. Then, I organize them into a full figure in PPT. But there are two problems that trouble me: one is the low resolution, and the other is the inconvenience when converting from one layout to another. When I read the post “Drawing Vector Graphics For Publication Figures” published by Prof. Xi Chen, I realized there are some better methods. Besides, I’m impressed by simplicity and beauty of the figures drawn by Prof. Xi Chen. Previously, I thought of pictures merely as a means of presentation, with the core being the findings and story of the entire project. Therefore, I consider drawing figures to be a reluctant task, not mention to beautify it. But the fact is that beautiful figures are indispensable to attract readers’ attention to the content. So, why not treat drawing figures as artwork and enjoy the process!

How?

The video gives me a quick introduction about how to draw the figures for publication.

Later, I’ll update it to show how I use these methods to draw beautiful figures for my own project.

An example

  1. Set the size of the PPT slide according to the journal.
  2. Combine multiple sub-figures into a single figure in slide 1 when they illustrate the same idea.
  3. Use another slide 2 to add all text.
  4. Paste all the sub-figures in slide 1 and all the text in slide 2 into slide3 which will be exported as high-resolution (300 or 600 dpi) tiff to insert manuscript.

There are two key points to improve the flexibility and resolution: (1) separate the texts and sub-figures into two slides, so it’s more convenient for us to reorganize the figure according to different requirements; (2) high-resolution tiff format will be enough for publications even if it’s not vector graphic.
